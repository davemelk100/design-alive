The Lifestyle Quality Financial Management℠ (LQFM℠) and Lifestyle Quality Financial Advisor℠ (LQFA℠) training programs are created and administered by Cygnet Institute of Personal Financial Literacy, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization and available from Cygnet’s partner Rochester Christian University.

Terminal Learning Objective: Train veterans to become certified Lifestyle Quality Financial Advisors℠ who can provide fee-based retirement planning to households and serve low- to moderate-income communities through educational workshops at public libraries.

Training Overview

Training involves four primary activities:

  • Activity #1: Teach students the skills and behaviors needed to guide clients through the Cygnet Process and attain LQFM℠ certification (3 months).
  • Activity #2a: Study and master retirement planning needs in both accumulation and distribution phases to attain LQFA℠ certification (18 months).
  • Activity #2b: Partner with an experienced Certified Retirement Counselor® to process at least 10 clients through the 4-stage Cygnet Process.
  • Activity #3: Complete business development training using the FDIC Money Smart curriculum to build a fee-based retirement counseling practice.

Details of Activities

Activity #1 (3 Months): LQFM℠ Certificate — Mastering the Experiential Cygnet Process℠

This activity trains students in the use of the Cygnet Cash Flow Planning Process℠ — a structured 4-step experiential planning framework that has helped thousands of households since 1989.

Students are trained to guide clients through:

  • Cash Flow Management
  • Budgeting and Income Planning
  • 401(k), 403(b), 457, and IRA Rules
  • Income Taxes and Capital Gains
  • Investment Planning and Portfolio Strategies
  • Estate Planning, Trusts, and Wills
  • Retirement Income Planning
  • Social Security
  • Medicare, Medicaid, and Long-Term Care considerations

Upon successful completion of Stage 1, participants are certified as Lifestyle Quality Financial Managers℠ (LQFM℠).

Activity #2a (18 Months): LQFA℠ Certificate — Advanced Technical Retirement Training

Students learn the technical foundation required to serve clients as retirement specialists. The LQFA℠ study material utilizes the Certified Retirement Counselor® (CRC®) curriculum developed by the International Foundation for Retirement Education (InFRE®).

More information: https://www.infre.org

The CRC® curriculum — licensed by Cygnet for use in the LQFA℠ program — consists of five structured study areas:

  1. Fundamentals of Retirement Planning
  2. Fundamentals of Investments
  3. Fundamentals of Retirement Plan Design
  4. Fundamentals of Retirement Income Management
  5. Principles of Retirement Counseling and Education

Successful completion prepares students who meet InFRE® eligibility requirements to sit for the Certified Retirement Counselor® examination and receive the CRC® designation.

Eligibility includes either:

  • Bachelor’s degree (or higher) from an accredited institution AND two years of relevant retirement-related professional experience within the last five years
  • OR
  • High school diploma (or equivalent) AND five years of relevant retirement-related professional experience within the last seven years

Activity #2b (Concurrent with LQFA Training)

Students partner with an experienced Certified Retirement Counselor® (CRC®) to gain experience processing at least 10 clients through the 4-stage Cygnet Process.

The CRC® initially leads meetings while mentoring the student, explaining the rationale behind client interactions. Over time, the student takes a larger role using a coaching model while the CRC® remains present to ensure the Cygnet Process is executed correctly.

Activity #3 (3 Months): Establishing a Fee-Based Retirement Planning Practice

Stage 3 focuses on building and sustaining a professional retirement counseling practice using the Money Smart for Small Business (MSSB) curriculum developed by the U.S. Small Business Administration and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation.

Program information: https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-small-business

The MSSB instructor-led curriculum includes 13 modules:

  1. Business readiness and self-assessment
  2. Business planning and resource development
  3. Cash flow management
  4. Banking services
  5. Organizational structure (LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp, Sole Proprietor, Partnership)
  6. Time management
  7. Financial management
  8. Record keeping systems
  9. Credit reporting
  10. Risk management
  11. Insurance planning
  12. Tax planning and reporting
  13. Succession planning and exit strategy
